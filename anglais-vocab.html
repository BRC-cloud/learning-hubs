Lesson 6 - Task 1 : Oral Pitch (3 questions)

Fait - L8

Prepare 3 reponses, dis-les 3 fois a voix haute, enregistre la 3eme. Pas de notes le jour J.

Pourquoi c'est difficile : Tu as le contenu dans la tete, mais le passage ecrit → oral bloque. La methode : 1) Comprendre la structure 2) Reduire a des mots-cles 3) Parler depuis les mots-cles 4) Repeter jusqu'a l'automatisme.

Q1 : "Tell me about yourself and your background"

Structure : Qui + Formation → Experience cle → Ce que je cherche

Mots-cles a retenir : materials engineer, background, Aptar Group, industrial R&D, material development, formulation, processing, characterisation, surface treatments, led, from scratch, industrialisation, patent applications, transition into, aeronautics

Technique : Imagine que tu rencontres quelqu'un a une conference. 30 secondes max.
Piege #1 : ne dis pas "I lead" (present) mais "I led" (passe - c'est fini).
Piege #2 : ne dis pas "I make testings" mais "I ran tests" / "I carried out tests".

Ta version (travaille-la ici) :

Modele : "My name is Caother Berriache. I am a materials engineer with a background in chemistry and materials science. At Aptar Group, I worked in industrial R&D on two main areas: firstly, material development, including formulation, processing and characterisation, and secondly, surface treatments, where I led a coating development from scratch through to industrialisation. This work led to two patent applications. I am now looking to transition into surface treatment roles in aeronautics."

Q2 : "What has your experience with surface treatments been so far?"

Structure : Projet concret + chiffre → Activites quotidiennes → Transferabilite

Mots-cles : led, surface treatment, industrial context, resistance 20 to 100%, R&D activities, material selection, characterisation, method not material

Le chiffre qui tue : "I improved the resistance from 20 up to 100 percent." Les recruteurs retiennent les chiffres.
La phrase finale : "What changes is the material, not the method." ← ca montre la transferabilite.

Ta version :

Modele : "At Aptar, I led a surface treatment development project in an industrial context. I ran various tests and improved the resistance of the treated materials from 20 up to 100 percent. Beyond this specific project, I also supported day-to-day R&D activities: material selection, characterisation, and qualification. The skills I built transfer directly across materials - what changes is the material, not the method."

Q3 : "Why are you interested in [company] specifically?"

Structure : Fait concret sur l'entreprise → Lien avec ton expertise → Ce que TU apportes

Mots-cles : I have been following, what interests me, confident, productive quickly, testing methodology, analytical approach, I would bring

Piege principal : Ne pas parler de ce que le poste T'apporte, mais de ce que TU apportes a EUX.
Test : Remplace le nom de l'entreprise par un concurrent. Si ca marche encore, c'est trop generique. Ajoute un fait precis.
Adapte a chaque entreprise : Ce template est un squelette. Pour Thales : cite le sujet de recherche vitrimer/eau de mer.

Ta version :

Exemple Thales : "I have been following Thales's work on materials for naval defence systems. What interests me is the research topic: studying how vitrimer materials age in seawater combines polymer science, which is my background, with a real industrial challenge. I am confident I can be productive quickly because the testing methodology and analytical approach I developed at Aptar transfer directly. I would bring a strong experimental background and a genuine drive to tackle complex material challenges."

Methode de pratique en 5 etapes

Etape 1 : Lis tes 3 reponses a voix haute 2 fois (lentement)
Etape 2 : Cache le texte. Regarde seulement les mots-cles. Parle.
Etape 3 : Sans rien. Dis ta reponse 3 fois. Enregistre la 3eme.
Etape 4 : Reecoute : tu dis "led" ou "lead" ? "tests" ou "testings" ? Le chiffre 20-100% est la ?
Etape 5 : Utilise le Oral Drill (onglet "Oral") pour simuler avec timer.

Lesson 7 - Task 2 : Questions indirectes

Fait - corrige en L8

Reecris ces 5 questions directes en utilisant les 3 ouvertures polies.

Les 3 ouvertures a memoriser :
1. "Could you tell me..." → + phrase en ordre NORMAL (pas interrogatif)
2. "I was wondering..." → + phrase en ordre normal
3. "Do you mind me asking..." → + phrase en ordre normal

Le piege : En francais on garde l'ordre interrogatif. En anglais indirect, on revient a l'ordre normal.
"What does the role involve?" → "Could you tell me what the role involves?" (PAS "what does the role involve")
Question directeTa version indirecte
What projects would I work on?
When will you contact me with a decision?
How big is the team?
What does the onboarding process look like?
Is there a possibility of a permanent contract?
  1. "Could you tell me what projects I would work on?"
  2. "I was wondering when you might contact me with a decision."
  3. "Do you mind me asking how big the team is?"
  4. "Could you tell me what the onboarding process looks like?"
  5. "I was wondering whether there might be a possibility of a permanent contract."

Lesson 8 - Task 1 : CAR structure (3 reponses)

En cours

Ecris 3 reponses d'entretien en structure CAR (Context, Action, Result). 5-7 phrases chacune. Utilise le past perfect au moins 1 fois dans chaque contexte.

La formule past perfect : Before [past perfect] ... I [past simple] ... which meant that [result].
L'histoire du testing panel couvre les 3 : ecris 3 versions avec des angles legerement differents.

Q1 : "Tell me about a time you took initiative."

Angle : Tu as cree un outil (le panel d'immersion chimique) qui n'existait pas.

Past perfect : "Before I introduced this method, each material had been tested individually, which was time-consuming."
Idiom : "I went the extra mile to build something that did not exist yet."

Ta version :

Modele : "I took the initiative to develop a chemical immersion panel that allowed my team to test multiple materials simultaneously and select the most suitable approach for improving chemical resistance. Before I introduced this method, each material had been tested individually, which was time-consuming. I went the extra mile to build something that did not exist yet, which meant that the results we achieved were both reliable and reproducible."

Q2 : "Tell me about a challenge you overcame."

Angle : Le probleme de resistance chimique + la methodo que tu as developpee.

Past perfect : "Because no methodology had existed for this material, I developed one from scratch."

Ta version :

Modele : "When I started the coating project at Aptar, no standardised testing method had been established for this material. I had to design my own approach from scratch. I carried out a literature review, identified CVD as the most suitable technique, and coordinated external partners for the deposition. The compliance rate improved from 20 to 100 percent, which meant the project could move towards industrialisation."

Q3 : "Tell me about a project you are proud of."

Angle : Le resultat complet : du POC a l'industrialisation.

Past perfect : "The results I achieved would not have been possible had I not redesigned the testing approach first."

Ta version :

Modele : "The project I am most proud of is the CVD coating development I led at Aptar. Before I joined the team, no standardised testing method had been established for this material. I took the initiative to develop one, coordinated external partners, and brought the compliance rate from 20 to 100 percent. The results would not have been possible had I not redesigned the testing approach first. This work led to two patent applications and the project moved towards industrialisation."

Lesson 8 - Task 3 : "Go the extra mile" sentence

En cours

Ecris 1 phrase avec l'idiome "to go the extra mile" que tu pourrais dire naturellement en entretien, connectee a ton experience de stage. Dis-la 5 fois a voix haute.

Definition : To make more effort than is expected or required. To do something beyond what was asked.
Ton lien : Perfectionnisme + initiative au stage = aller au-dela de ce qui etait demande.

Ta phrase :

Exemple : "When no testing methodology existed for her material, she went the extra mile and designed her own panel from scratch rather than waiting for someone to provide one."
