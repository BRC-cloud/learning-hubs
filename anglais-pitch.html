Task 1 - Oral Pitch

Lessons 6 & 7 | 3 questions to answer out loud - no notes, no reading

Q1. Tell me about yourself and your background.
Your polished 30-second pitch
My name is Caother Berriache. I am a materials engineer with a background in chemistry and materials science. At Aptar Group, I worked in industrial R&D on two main areas: material development, including formulation, processing and characterisation, and surface treatments, where I led a coating development from scratch through to industrialisation. This work led to two patent applications. I am now looking to transition into surface treatment roles in aeronautics.
Piege : I lead a project = present. I led a project = past. Jamais dire internship - dire "at Aptar Group".
Strategie : Rester generique (surface treatments, material development). Preciser polymere/CVD seulement si on te pose la question.
Q2. What has your experience with surface treatments been so far?
Use present perfect + present perfect continuous
At Aptar, I led a surface treatment development project in an industrial context. I ran various tests and improved the resistance of the treated materials from 20 up to 100 percent. Beyond this specific project, I also supported day-to-day R&D activities: material selection, characterisation, and qualification. The skills I built transfer directly across materials - what changes is the material, not the method.
Piege : I make different testings → I ran various tests / I carried out tests. "Testings" n'existe pas.
Le chiffre qui tue : "improved resistance from 20 up to 100 percent" - c'est concret, c'est ce que les recruteurs retiennent. Le dire a chaque fois.
Q3. Why are you interested in [company] specifically?
Research one real fact about the company and mention it
[Exemple Thales]
I have been following Thales's work in [fait concret : programme, techno, contrat]. What interests me is [lien avec ton expertise]. I am confident I can be productive quickly because the testing methodology and analytical approach I developed at Aptar transfer directly. I would bring [ce que TU apportes].
Piege : You have to know we worked a lot of times → I would like to point out that we covered all of this extensively. "You have to know" est trop direct.
it's not a big deal → I am confident I can be productive quickly (registre pro)

Methode d'apprentissage (7 jours)

1
Comprendre (1 fois) 10 min
Lis les 3 reponses sur la fiche. Surligne les mots que tu hesites a prononcer. Cherche la prononciation sur Google ("how to pronounce industrialisation").
2
Lire a voix haute (jours 1-2) 5 min/jour
Lis chaque reponse a voix haute 3 fois en regardant la fiche. Lentement. Articule. Pas besoin de memoriser encore.
3
Cacher et parler (jours 3-4) 5 min/jour
Retourne la fiche. Reponds a chaque question avec TES mots. C'est pas du par coeur - c'est les idees dans l'ordre. Regarde la fiche apres pour verifier les pieges (lead/led, testings, etc.).
4
S'enregistrer (jours 5-6) 5 min/jour
Dis chaque reponse 3 fois. Enregistre-toi sur la 3eme. Reecoute : est-ce que tu as dit "led" ou "lead" ? "tests" ou "testings" ? Le chiffre 20-100% est la ?
5
Simulation (jour 7) 5 min
Debout, sans fiche, sans enregistrement. Imagine que le recruteur est devant toi. Enchaine les 3 questions. Si tu bloques, c'est OK - reformule, ne t'arrete pas.
Checklist avant la lecon 8 :
  • Je sais repondre aux 3 questions sans lire
  • J'ai un enregistrement de la 3eme repetition
  • Je dis "led" pas "lead", "tests" pas "testings"
  • Je cite le chiffre 20-100% dans Q2
  • J'ai un fait reel sur l'entreprise pour Q3
