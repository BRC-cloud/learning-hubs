"I hold myself to very high standards" came out naturally in conversation, replacing "exigeant". That is fluency building in real time.

Key corrections to remember

fewer (countable) vs less (uncountable): fewer tests, less time

phenomena is already plural (not phenomenols/phenomenons)

shortcut is a noun — the verb is to shorten

implicated = involved in a crime. Use invested or committed

Idiom of the day

To be on the same page — to have the same understanding of a situation, plan, or idea as someone else.

"Before the team started the experiment, the lead engineer made sure everyone was on the same page about the testing methodology."

Teacher's note on calques

Your brain currently does: thought in French → translate → write in English. That is normal at B2. The goal is: thought → English directly.

The fastest way to stop translating is to read and listen to more English, not to study more grammar. Every episode of Malcolm in the Middle, every Chatter Learning session: your brain absorbing English patterns directly.

Three tenses to master
The one question to ask before every sentence:
Still relevant today? → present perfect
Still doing this right now? → present perfect continuous
Happened before something else in my story? → past perfect
Active

Present Perfect

▼
have / has + past participle

A past action still relevant now, or accumulated experience

Signal wordsExample
already, yet, justI have developed a chemical immersion panel.
never, everI have never worked in aeronautics before.
since, forI have studied English since February.
Active

Present Perfect Continuous

▼
have / has + been + verb-ing

An ongoing action that started in the past and is still happening

Signal wordsExample
for, sinceI have been applying to companies for three months.
all week, recentlyI have been studying English every week since February.
Active

Past Perfect

▼
had + past participle

An action that happened before another past action

Signal wordsExample
before, alreadyBefore I started testing, I had already completed a literature review.
by the timeBy the time I joined, no proof of concept had existed yet.
becauseBecause no method had existed, I took the initiative.
